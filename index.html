About

A maker who learns by building the next version

I'm a student engineer and builder. I like problems I can hold in my hands — things that move, sense, or react — and I learn fastest by getting a rough version working, then improving it.

Most of what I make sits where electronics, code, and fabrication meet. I sketch an idea, wire up a prototype on a breadboard, print the parts I need, and keep revising until it behaves the way I pictured. The versions on this site (v1 → v2) are exactly that process in the open.

  • Engineering and how mechanical and electronic parts work together
  • Computer science and writing the code that controls hardware
  • Biology and science experiments
  • Arduino and electronics
  • 3D printing and fabrication
  • Product design and how a build feels to use
  • Experimentation and iterative improvement
